# Unit Economics
## What A Converted Lead Is Actually Worth

The audit itself isn’t really the business.

The valuable part is identifying startups that are already spending serious money on AI tooling and are likely to need:
- infrastructure financing
- GPU credits
- SaaS financing
- AI spend optimization later on

The assumption here is pretty simple:

If a startup is already spending somewhere around ₹40k–₹2L/month on AI tools, there’s a decent chance they’ll eventually spend much more on infrastructure or operational tooling too.

So the audit is basically a way to surface high-intent companies early.

## Rough Math

The numbers are obviously rough, but the economics can still be reasoned about.

Assume:
- 10 consultation calls
- 1 converts into a meaningful relationship
- average customer value ≈ $5k/year

then:

```txt
one consultation call ≈ $500 expected value
```

If 20% of completed audits turn into consultation calls

then:

```txt
one completed audit ≈ $100 expected value
```

That’s enough to justify spending real effort on distribution if the traffic quality is strong.


# CAC By Channel

The initial GTM strategy intentionally prioritizes channels with near-zero acquisition cost.

## 1. Founder / Communities
Estimated CAC: ~$0–$5
Mostly just time and consistency.

This channel works because the audience already discusses:

- AI tooling
- engineering workflows
- startup costs
- SaaS spend


## 2. Reply-Driven Distribution On X

The idea is to reply to discussions like:

- “What AI tools does your team use?”
- “Cursor vs Copilot?”
- “AI tooling costs are getting insane”

threads with mini-audits and screenshots.

Estimated CAC: ~$5–$15

depending on how much effort goes into distribution.

## 3. Viral Report Sharing
Every shared audit already contains:

- savings estimates
- recommendations
- branded previews
- a public report link

Estimated CAC: effectively near $0
if the reports are genuinely useful enough to circulate internally.

# What Profitability Could Look Like

Assumptions:

100 completed audits
- 20 consultation calls
- 2 customers
- ≈ $10k ARR

If it costs less than roughly $1k–$2k to acquire those 100 audits, the funnel starts looking pretty healthy. 

# What Needs To Be True To Reach ~$1M ARR

Assumption:

- Average customer value: $5k ARR

Then reaching $1M ARR means roughly 200 active customers

Working backward:
200 customers
- 2,000 consultation calls
- 10,000 completed audits

Spread across 18 months, that’s roughly ~550 completed audits/month.

Honestly, that doesn’t feel impossible if the sharing loop works.
